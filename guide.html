1. Getting Started

  • Download from the App Store. Search for "Kanshu" or use the link on the homepage.
  • First launch: pick your HSK level. You'll be asked to choose your level, from Beginner through HSK 6+. This calibrates the coach and word difficulty highlights.
  • Don't worry about picking wrong. The coach adapts to your actual reading behavior over time. Your initial choice just sets a starting point.
  • Your first book. Tap "Add Book" to import a file, paste text, or load the built-in sample text to start reading immediately.

2. Importing Books

  • EPUB files — The best format. Preserves chapter structure via TOC metadata, giving you proper chapter navigation and page breaks.
  • Plain text (.txt) — Simple import. Chapters are detected automatically by pattern matching (e.g., 第一章, Chapter 1).
  • PDF — Text is extracted via PDFKit. Works well for most Chinese PDFs, though scanned documents are not supported.
  • Web URLs — Paste any Chinese article URL. The app supports GB18030 encoding, so it handles most Chinese websites correctly.
  • Paste — Copy text from anywhere and paste it directly into Kanshu. Great for short articles or excerpts.
Tip: EPUB gives the best experience with proper chapter navigation. If you have a choice of format, always go with EPUB.

3. Reading

  • Page navigation. Swipe left or right, or tap the edges of the screen. The page curl animation follows the Apple Books style.
  • Tap any word for an instant lookup — pinyin, definition, HSK level, and frequency data appear in a popover.
  • Page indicator at the bottom shows your current position and overall progress through the book.
  • Bookmarks. Tap the bookmark icon to save your current page. Access all bookmarks from the bottom menu.
  • Highlights. Long-press to enter selection mode, then drag to highlight text. Choose from 4 highlight colors.
  • Full-text search. Open the bottom menu and tap the search icon to find any word or phrase across the entire book.

4. Pinyin Mode

Toggle "Show Pinyin" in the reader settings panel (tap the gear icon). When enabled, pinyin annotations appear above every word with tone coloring:

  • Tone 1 (high) — red
  • Tone 2 (rising) — orange
  • Tone 3 (dip) — green
  • Tone 4 (falling) — blue
  • Neutral tone — gray

This mode is especially useful for beginners or when encountering unfamiliar texts. The reader automatically adjusts the layout to accommodate the extra height needed for pinyin.

5. Themes & Customization

Open the reader settings panel (gear icon) to customize your reading experience.

  • Themes: Sepia (warm, default), Light (clean white), Dark (night reading).
  • Font: Songti (serif, traditional feel), PingFang (modern sans-serif), Kaiti (calligraphic).
  • Font size: Adjustable from 16pt to 32pt.
  • Line spacing: 1.0x to 2.0x for comfortable reading.
  • Margins: Small, Medium, or Large.
  • Highlight coloring: None, HSK Level (color words by difficulty), or Saved Words (highlight words you've saved).

6. Word Lookup

Tap any word in the reader to open the word popover. It shows:

  • Chinese characters, pinyin, and English definition. Pinyin is tone-colored for easy reading.
  • Tags: HSK level (1-6), part of speech, book frequency, and how many times you've encountered this word.
  • "Grammar & examples" button — generates context-aware grammar notes with example sentences.
  • "Ask AI" button — uses Apple Intelligence (iOS 26+) for deeper explanations including part-of-speech analysis, usage examples, and full sentence breakdown.
  • "Sentence" button — translates the full sentence using Apple Translation or cloud AI.
  • "+" button — saves the word to your word list for spaced repetition review.

7. Segmentation Engines

Kanshu offers three different engines for splitting Chinese text into individual words. Each has different strengths.

CC-CEDICT (Dictionary-based)

Uses a 120,000+ entry Chinese-English dictionary with a bidirectional maximum matching algorithm. The most reliable option for common vocabulary. Includes pinyin, definitions, and HSK levels for every word it recognizes.

Apple NL (Default)

Uses Apple's built-in NLTokenizer for word segmentation. No extra dictionary files needed. Good accuracy backed by Apple's NLP models. Falls back to the CEDICT dictionary for definitions and pinyin lookup.

Jieba (Experimental)

Statistical segmentation using Hidden Markov Models via the cppjieba C++ library. Approximately 97% accuracy. Requires bundling additional dictionary files. Best for handling new words and slang not in the standard dictionary.

To switch engines, go to Settings (gear icon in the library) and select your preferred Segmentation Engine. All engines produce tappable word tokens for definitions.

8. Grammar & AI

Kanshu uses a three-tier system for grammar explanations, with automatic fallback between tiers:

Tier How It Works Requirements
Bundled Pre-generated grammar notes for 20,000+ patterns. Instant results, fully offline. None (always available)
On-device AI Apple Intelligence generates context-aware grammar explanations in 1-2 seconds. No data leaves your device. iOS 26+ and supported hardware
Cloud AI Rich, contextual explanations in 1-3 seconds. Supports Anthropic Claude, OpenAI, Google Gemini, or any OpenAI-compatible endpoint. API key required

The app tries each tier in order: Bundled, then On-device, then Cloud. If one tier is unavailable, it automatically falls back to the next.

To set up Cloud AI, go to Settings, then Cloud AI, and enter your API key and select your provider.

9. Reading Coach

The reading coach is an adaptive system that learns from your reading behavior and generates targeted exercises.

  • Silent observation. The coach watches silently for the first 8 pages, tracking which words you tap, which grammar you look up, which sentences you translate, and how long you spend on each page.
  • Targeted challenges. When it detects you're struggling with a grammar pattern, it generates mini-challenges. Types include: multiple choice, tone quiz, grammar role identification, sentence reordering, and vocabulary recall.
  • Pattern mastery progresses through levels: Noticed, Improving, Stable, and finally Mastered.
  • View your progress in Pattern Radar (tap the insights button in the library).
  • Non-intrusive. The coach never interrupts more than 3 times per reading session, so it won't break your flow.

10. Spaced Repetition (SRS)

  • Save any word by tapping the "+" button in the word popover.
  • Review cards via the flashcard icon in the library view.
  • SM-2 algorithm. Cards are scheduled based on how well you remembered them. The spacing increases as you get better at a word.
  • Rating scale: Again (forgot), Hard, Good, Easy. Rate honestly for the best results.
  • Daily review. Cards resurface on their due date. A few minutes of daily review keeps words in long-term memory.
  • Export to Pleco. Export your word list in Pleco format for use with other Chinese learning apps.

11. Table of Contents & Navigation

  • EPUBs: Chapters are extracted from the book's built-in TOC metadata for accurate navigation.
  • Text files: Chapters are detected via pattern matching (第一章, Chapter 1, etc.).
  • Access: Open the bottom menu and tap Contents to see all chapters, bookmarks, and highlights with page numbers.
  • Page breaks. Chapters start on fresh pages, so you always get a clean break between sections.

12. Pattern Radar (Insights)

Pattern Radar shows all grammar patterns you've encountered, grouped by mastery level:

Mastered Stable Improving Unstable Noticed
  • Each pattern shows: name, confidence percentage, tier (A/B/C), and trend direction.
  • "Analyze deeper" uses AI to generate a summary of your strengths and weak areas across all patterns.
  • Focus Areas at the top of the screen highlight patterns that need the most attention.

13. Tips & Tricks

  • Enter selection mode. Long-press a word to start highlighting text.
  • Dismiss the word popover. Swipe down on it or tap outside.
  • Give the coach time. It works best after reading 20+ pages, since it needs data to learn your patterns.
  • Use EPUB format for the best reading experience with proper chapter structure.
  • Export word lists to Pleco for flashcard study on the go with other apps.
  • Fully offline. The app works completely offline. Cloud AI and Apple Intelligence are optional enhancements that add depth but are not required.
